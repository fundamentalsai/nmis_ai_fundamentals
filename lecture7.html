Chapters

Chapter 1: Introduction to Regression problem and different types of Input data

This segment explores the complexities of handling various types of input variables in Regression problems. Inputs can include numbers, features, categories, text descriptions, and images. An example involves predicting the optimal price of a used car for a company like Auto Trader. Various car attributes such as production place, capacity, age, maintenance frequency, and color must be considered. Effective interpolation methods are needed to account for the impact of these diverse attributes, including non-numerical data, on the car's price.

Chapter 2: Approaches to Handling Diverse Data Types

This segment discusses strategies for managing different types of input data in neural networks. The discussion includes using Python to build complex models beyond sequential ones and inputting data in various formats like single numbers, matrices, or feature compositions. There's no single correct solution in neural networks; success depends on experience and chosen approaches. The segment also touches on the importance of categorizing data strategically, with meaningful thresholds for features such as mileage or age.

Watch Chapter 2

Chapter 3: Techniques for Digitizing Text: Tokenization and Frequency Encoding

This segment explains the process of digitizing text using tokenization and frequency-based encoding. It discusses how to compress and efficiently process data by assigning numeric values to words based on their frequency within a text.

Watch Chapter 3

Chapter 4: Challenges and Techniques in Text Digitization and Tokenization

The segment discusses the process of digitizing and tokenizing text for neural network processing. It highlights the challenge of representing similar words with unique numerical tokens based on their frequency. This can lead to varying lengths of tokenized text, which complicates input data uniformity for neural networks. To address this, the "bag of words" technique is introduced, which converts text into a fixed-size vector, ensuring consistent input sizes for neural network training and processing.

Watch Chapter 4

Chapter 5: Discussion on Text Digitization and Tokenization Techniques

The segment discusses methods for digitizing and normalizing text data, focusing on the "bag of words" technique. Proper training can help neural networks recognize word relationships and handle misspellings. The next session will address regression problems and word sequence issues in texts.
