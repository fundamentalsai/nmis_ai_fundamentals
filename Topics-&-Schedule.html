Welcome to Fundamentals of AI for Manufacturing

Dive into the transformative power of AI in manufacturing with our course "Fundamentals of AI for Manufacturing". This program demystifies AI, focusing on its foundational principles and practical applications in the industrial sector. Through theoretical insights and hands-on workshops, explore key modeling techniques and their impact on manufacturing processes. Ideal for managers, professionals, and non-coders, the course offers enriched explanations with examples, allowing participants to experiment with AI models. Join us to revolutionize your manufacturing strategies.

Prerequisites

This course has no prerequisites. Participants are not required to have prior knowledge of coding or artificial intelligence/machine learning (AI/ML). Designed with industry professionals in mind, this course aims to introduce the fundamentals of AI in manufacturing to those with expertise in the field, regardless of their background in technology. Whether you're new to AI/ML or seeking to understand its application in manufacturing, this course is tailored for you.

Structure of the Programme / Course Outline

We adopt a modular approach to the course design, ensuring a comprehensive and systematic exploration of key concepts in AI for manufacturing. A detailed mind map supporting this modular approach will be provided to facilitate your learning journey.

Lecture Outline

  1. The Basic Idea of Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)

    1st Part: Theory


    • Introduction to ANN: Definition, historical background, and its significance in manufacturing.
    • Fundamental Concepts: Neurons, layers, and how they mimic human brain functionality.
    • Applications: Real-world manufacturing case studies where ANN has been pivotal.

    2st Part: Technical Overview


    • Structure of ANN: Detailed explanation of input, hidden, and output layers.
    • Basic functioning: Activation functions, forward propagation, and error calculation.
    Workshop
    • Implementing a simple ANN model in Python using Google Colab.
    • Experimenting with different network architectures and observing performance changes on a manufacturing dataset.
